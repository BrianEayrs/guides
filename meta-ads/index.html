Meta Ads Setup

Run Your Meta Ads from the Terminal with Claude

Give Claude direct access to your own ad account through Meta's Marketing API. Once it is wired in, your terminal Claude can upload creative, write the copy, build campaigns to your spec, pull results, and launch, all on your approval. You set it up once. After that, running ads is a short prompt.

What you are building

Three parts, set up one time:

  • Full API access. A Meta developer app and a System User token that does not expire. That token is a key to your ad account. Claude uses it to talk to Meta directly.
  • A clean foundation. One naming pattern, server-side tracking, and saved audiences, so your data stays trustworthy and every campaign starts from the same base.
  • A reusable playbook. A short document holding your account IDs, naming, tracking, and defaults. Paste it at the start of any future session and Claude has full context in one message.

Before you start

  • A Meta Business account with an ad account and a payment method on file.
  • A Facebook Page, and an Instagram account if you run there.
  • Claude running in a terminal (Claude Code), so it can make the API calls itself.
  • Comfort creating a developer app in your browser and setting an environment variable. The prompt walks you through both, but you do the clicks.
  • Your creative on hand: the image or video files. Claude can write the copy.

How the access works

You create the token once in your browser. From then on, Claude runs the actual calls to Meta using that token: creating campaigns, uploading creative, reading results. You are not clicking through Ads Manager. You describe what you want, Claude builds it, and you approve before anything spends. Managing your own ad account this way does not need Meta's long app-review process. That only applies to managing other people's accounts at scale.

Treat the token like a password. It controls real money. Store it only as an environment variable. Never paste it into a chat, never put it in a file you commit to Git, and never let anything print it to the screen. If it ever leaks, delete it in Business Settings and generate a new one. The prompt below is built to respect all of this.

Step 1: The setup prompt

Paste this into your terminal Claude. It walks you from zero to full access, then sets your foundation and writes your playbook. Work one step at a time.

Copy into Claude 
You are setting me up to run my Meta (Facebook and Instagram) ads directly from this terminal, with full access to my own ad account through the Marketing API. I understand Claude and I am comfortable running commands and setting an environment variable. Walk me through this from zero, one step at a time, and wait for me to finish each step before moving on.

Security rules you must follow:
- My access token is like a password to my ad spend. Never print it, never write it into a file that could be committed, and never ask me to paste it into this chat.
- We store the token as an environment variable only. When you show commands that use it, refer to the variable, never the value.

## Part 1: Create the access (I do the clicks, you give exact steps)
1. Confirm I have a Meta Business account with an ad account and a payment method, plus a Facebook Page (and Instagram account if I have one).
2. Walk me through creating a Meta developer app of type Business at developers.facebook.com and adding the Marketing API product to it. Tell me where to find my App ID.
3. In Business Settings, walk me through creating a System User, assigning it to my ad account and Page with full control, and assigning my app to it.
4. Walk me through generating a token for that System User with the permissions ads_management, ads_read, and business_management, set to never expire.
5. Help me collect and write down everything EXCEPT the token: my ad account ID (act_...), Page ID, pixel or dataset ID, and Instagram account ID.

## Part 2: Wire the token into this terminal
- Give me the exact command to store my token as an environment variable named META_TOKEN, and how to make it permanent in my shell profile, without ever echoing the value.
- Then run one read-only test call to confirm access, such as listing the campaigns on my ad account. If it fails, help me fix the permissions before we go on.

## Part 3: Set the foundation
Once the test call works:
- Propose a naming pattern for campaigns, ad sets, and ads in lowercase_with_underscores that encodes objective, audience, and creative. Give three examples and use it on everything from now on.
- Check my pixel is firing on my key pages, and check whether I have server-side tracking (Conversions API) on my main events. If not, walk me through it. Browser-only tracking leaves the optimizer half-blind and gets worse over time.
- Confirm which standard events I fire (Lead, CompleteRegistration, Purchase, and so on) and which one each campaign type should optimize for.
- Help me define the audiences I will reuse: a cold audience, a retargeting audience (site visitors, video viewers, engagers), and a lookalike if I have a source.
- Set my default country or countries, age range, and a sensible starting daily budget.
- Confirm a consistent UTM standard on my ad links so I can match spend to leads and sales in my CRM.

## Part 4: Write my playbook
Produce one "Meta Ads Playbook" document with everything except the token: my account, Page, pixel, and Instagram IDs, the naming pattern with examples, my standard events and what to optimize for, my saved audiences, my defaults, and my UTM standard. Format it so I can paste it back at the start of any future session and you will have full context.

Then list the short prompts I can give you from here to build, launch, and manage campaigns.

Start with Part 1, step 1.

The output that matters is the playbook at the end. Save it where you can find it. Keep the token out of it.

Step 2: Keep your playbook

The playbook is the payoff. It is a short block of text holding everything Claude needs about your account, minus the token. Save it in a note, a doc, or a project file. At the start of any future ad session, paste it first. Then your request can be one line, because the context is already loaded.

Your prompt library

With access wired in and your playbook saved, these are the short prompts you reuse as your needs change. Paste your playbook first, then one of these. Fill in the brackets.

Build any campaign (leads, sales, traffic, messages)

Copy into Claude 
Using my Meta Ads Playbook above, build a new campaign, paused.
Objective: [leads / sales / traffic / messages / engagement].
Offer: [what it is].
Destination: [URL or lead form].
Daily budget: [amount]. Start date: [date].
Audience: [cold / retargeting / lookalike, or describe].
Creative: [the file path or attach it, plus headline and main text].
Follow my naming pattern, optimize for my standard event for this objective, and show me a full summary before anything goes live. Do not launch until I say "launch it," then set only the campaign to active.

Retarget a warm audience

Copy into Claude 
Using my Playbook, build a paused retargeting campaign to people who [visited my page / watched 50%+ of my video / engaged in the last 30 days].
Offer: [what]. Daily budget: [amount].
Reuse my naming and tracking. Summarize before launch, and do not go live until I approve.

Scale a winner

Copy into Claude 
Pull the last 7 days for my active campaigns and tell me which ad set has the best cost per result. If one clearly leads, propose a plan to scale it (raise its budget slowly, or duplicate it into a fresh audience) and explain the risk of each. Change nothing until I approve.

Weekly report

Copy into Claude 
Pull my Meta results for the last 7 days: spend, results, cost per result, and the best and worst ad in each campaign. Flag anything trending the wrong way and suggest one change for each. Keep it plain language.

Pause the losers

Copy into Claude 
Find ad sets that have spent more than [amount] with zero or very expensive results in the last [number] days. List them with the numbers. Once I approve, pause only those and leave everything else running.

Why full access is worth the setup

A plug-in connector can manage campaigns by chat, but it stops where its built-in tools stop. A token and the Marketing API give Claude the whole surface: uploading your own video and image creative, building every part of a campaign, pulling any report, and running it all on a schedule if you want. It does not expire, so you set it once. And because Claude runs the calls from your terminal, you can grow it into your own tooling later: a daily results pull, bulk creative uploads, whatever you need. The setup takes an afternoon. It pays back every campaign after.

Wire in the token once, keep your playbook, and running Meta ads becomes a one-line request. Guard the token like the password it is.
